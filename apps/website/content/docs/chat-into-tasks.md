The point of a chat that can see your workspace is that it can also change it. When you've talked something through, ask for the result — the AI writes it into Companion rather than back at you.

## From conversation to work

Ask it to "make tasks for the launch we just discussed", and it creates them: real tasks, with titles and due dates, sitting in your Tasks list a second later. Ask it to draft the note and it creates that too. Everything it makes is an ordinary object you can edit, file, or delete like anything else.

It works in the other direction as well. "What's overdue?", "what did I decide about pricing?", "which notes link to the launch plan?" — the chat can search, follow links, and read your notes to answer.

## Check its work

The AI doesn't ask before it writes, so read what it made. Tasks land in **Unsorted** unless you tell it otherwise, which makes them easy to look over: open Tasks, scan the list, fix the dates it guessed wrong.

Dates are the usual place it drifts — "early next week" means something specific to it. Worth a glance.

> **Tip:** Ask it to file what it creates ("put them in the v1.2 launch project") and you skip the sorting step.
