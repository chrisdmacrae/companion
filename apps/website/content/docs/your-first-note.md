Notes are where your ideas live in Companion. They're plain text you can write freely, link to each other, and turn into tasks when you're ready to act. Here's how to create your first one.

## Create a note

From anywhere in the app, start a new note in one of three ways — whichever fits your flow:

1. Press `N` anywhere, or click the **＋ New note** button in the sidebar.
2. Give it a title on the first line — this becomes how the note is referenced everywhere else.
3. Keep typing. Notes save automatically — there's no save button to hunt for.

> **Tip:** Notes support Markdown. Type `## Heading`, `- item` for lists, or wrap text in `**bold**` — it renders as you go.

## Link notes together

Companion is built around connections. To link to another note, type `[[` and start searching for its title. Pick a match and Companion inserts a live link both ways — the note you referenced now shows this one under its **linked notes**.

These links are what power [the graph](/docs/using-the-graph): a map of how your ideas relate. Revisiting one note surfaces everything it touches, so nothing you write gets lost.

## Turn a note into tasks

When a note starts to feel like a plan, you don't have to retype it. Select any lines and choose **Make tasks**, or ask Companion in chat — the AI reads the note and creates checkable tasks linked back to their source.

Every task keeps a reference to the note it came from, so you always know the "why" behind the "what."

## Organize with tags

Add a tag anywhere in a note by typing `#` followed by a word, like `#reading` or `#idea`. Tags cut across folders, letting you gather related notes no matter where they live.

That's everything you need for your first note. Next, try [your first task](/docs/your-first-task) or explore [using the graph](/docs/using-the-graph).
