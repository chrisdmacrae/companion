Notes and tasks pile up fast. Projects are how you gather the ones that belong together — and areas are how you group the projects.

## Areas and projects

In the sidebar, click **＋** beside **Projects** to create an *area*: a broad, long-running part of your life. "Work". "Home". Then click **＋** beside the area to add a *project* inside it — something with an end, like "v1.2 launch".

![The sidebar with an area and a project, and the project's own view](/docs/project-view.png)

Open a project and you get its own view: the notes and tasks filed under it, the area it belongs to, and a progress ring that fills as its tasks get checked off.

## Filing things

Anything can be filed after the fact — there's no need to decide up front. With a note or task open, click the **folder** icon in its toolbar and tick the projects it belongs to. You can also drag a note or task from the list straight onto a project in the sidebar, and select several at once to file them in one go.

Nothing has to be filed at all. The **Unsorted** filter at the top of Notes and Tasks is precisely the pile of things you haven't sorted — a useful place to work from, not a mess to feel bad about.

> **Tip:** Deleting a project doesn't delete its contents by default — the notes and tasks inside simply become unsorted again.

## What about habits?

Habits are in the app's plumbing but not yet in your hands: the **Habits** tab is a placeholder while streaks and gentle nudges get built. Until then, a repeating [task](/docs/your-first-task) is the closest thing.
